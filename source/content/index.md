### The Building Blocks

  Every word is made of parts. Those parts are called morphemes — the smallest units of meaning.

  There are two kinds: 

  ---
 #### Roots
---
	The core of a word. It carries the main meaning.
	-dox- means belief
	-morph- means shape2*
	-path- means feeling
	A root can't usually stand alone — it needs help.

  ---
 #### Affixes
---
	  Pieces that attach to roots to change or add meaning. There are three types:

| Type   | Position        | Example         |
| ------ | --------------- | --------------- |
| Prefix | before the root | un-happy        |
| Suffix | after the root  | happiness       |
| Infix  | inside the root | rare in English |
0
 ---
 #### Put together
---
	un  +  happi  +  ness
	↑         ↑              ↑
	prefix  root   suffix
	= the state of not being happy  
	English drops the y and replaces it with i before most suffixes
	happy → happiness, happily, happier

 #### Roots
---
	Greek and Latin roots are often written with slightly varied spellings, as shown below.
	The hyphen indicates the most typical connecting vowel (phon-o, hydr-o, etc.)

| Part   | Meaning(s)     | Exemplars                                |
| ------ | -------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| astro  | stars, heavens | astronaut, astrology, astronomer         |
| bi-o   | life           | biography, biosphere, biology            |

| therm  | heat, warm     | thermostat, thermal, exothermic          |

| hom-o  | same, alike    | homonym, homogenize, homophone           |
| hydr-o | water          | hydrogen, hydrology, hydroelectric       |

 #### Suffixes
---

| Part           | Meaning(s)                 | Exemplars                              |
| -------------- | -------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| -al            | relating to                | bacterial, theatrical, natural         |

| -phobia        | abnormal fear of           | acrophobia, claustraphobia, xenophobia |
| -scribe/script | to write                   | transcript, describe, manuscript       |
| -sophy/sophic  | wisdom, knowledge          | philosophy, theosophy, antroposophic   |
| -dox           | belief                     | orthodox, paradox, heterodox           |

---
 #### Rules of thumb
---
	Try to match language origins so Greek + Greek and Latin + Latin
	Consider if the meaning behind your word already exists in another word.

 #### Examples
---

"Autoparadoxy"
  - auto- = self
  - para- = against
  - -dox- = belief
  - -y = state/condition
  - Meaning: Self-contradicting belief
